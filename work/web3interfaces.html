Context

There has been an uptrend in discussion surrounding user interface and user experience design across web3. Whilst there has been an increase in talented designers making the transition from web2 to web3 in the past year, there is still a lot of misunderstanding regarding the topic and lack of recognition of current design teams and projects.

Project Details

I started Web3 Interfaces on Twitter as a way to showcase the user interfaces that were being designed across web3. Initially it was a way for me to be able to identify the current trends in UI in the space, though I felt that I wanted to publicise my research on Twitter as a way for designers and web3 enthusiasts alike to recognise the work that is being done in the space at the moment.

I truly believe that UI design in web3 has improved immensely in the past few years and that through learning from other projects, there is an opportunity to form shared understanding around what it means to design in this industry. I also think provides inspiration and guidance to those who are looking to start designing for web3 platforms.

https://twitter.com/web3interfaces

Vision of the Future

My vision for this project is to inspire future and current web3 UI designers to engage in the space and be able to acknowledge the trends which can be applied to their own designs. I feel that this has the potential to expand beyond Twitter posts to creation of a community, running workshops or engaging in Twitter spaces or podcast discussions about what I or others have identified designing in web3 and what this means for the platforms that will be created for users in the future.
